## Core Principles

- Show impact, don't just list duties. Every bullet should answer "so what?"
- Use numbers only when they are accurate and verifiable from your own resume.
- Write like you're explaining to a smart colleague — not performing for a committee.
- "We" for team work; always add one clause explaining your specific role within it.

## Hard Rules — Things to Never Do

- No percentages unless the exact figure is in your base resume.
- No Oxford commas. No double dashes (em dash or nothing).
- Never start a bullet with: responsible for, tasked with, assisted with, helped with.
- No generic buzzwords. Replace:
    "leveraged"  → "used"
    "utilized"   → "used"
    "synergy"    → delete
    "innovative" → delete
    "results-driven" → delete

## Bullet Structure

- Start every bullet with a strong past-tense action verb.
- Lead with the outcome when possible: "Reduced X by doing Y" beats "Did Y, reducing X".
- Keep bullets to 1–2 lines. Break anything longer into two bullets.
- Name specific tools and systems (e.g. "Terraform" not "infrastructure-as-code tool").

## Quantification Policy

- Include real numbers when available.
- When no number exists, use qualitative scale words: significantly, measurably,
  noticeably, dramatically, consistently, effectively, reliably.
- Never fabricate a figure — it creates risk in interviews.
